One day, while Patricia is working in her garden, she hears a faint buzzing. She spots from across the yard a large swarm of bees in her tree on her property. She is a little scared but she knows that bees are important to our environment and knows that she doesn't want to harm them, she just doesn't want them in her yard.

She takes to her computer and starts by searching for local bee removal services. She finds a local Beekeeping Association that has a hotline specifically for swarms and gives them a call.

The next day an amateur beekeeper named Scott comes to her home and carefully removes the swarm. He tells Patricia that they will be going to a local apiary with another member of the Portland Metro Beekeeping Association who frequently volunteers to rescue swarms.

Over the next few days, Patricia is thinking about all of the things Scott told her about bees. He really seemed to love them and knew quite a lot about them. She knows that they are important for our ecosystem and wants to learn a little more...and maybe talk to someone about keeping her own bees.

She logs onto the Portland Metro Beekeeping association like Scott recommended and reads lots of very informative and interesting facts about bees and beekeeping. She starts to think that maybe it wouldn't be soo hard for her to start keeping bees for herself.

Patricia decides that she wants to learn more about keeping bees and to stay up to date with all the information her local association may publish. She also wants to support the work the volunteers like Scott do, so she signs up to become a member.
