PeculiAR

Product Design

True crime and macabre history enthusiasts want a place where they can access local information about true crime and unusual or creepy history. PeculiAR is a platform that offers access to local historical information with an AR component to create a fun and immersive learning adventure, while cultivating a sense of respect for crime victims and the users’ environment.

Research

Our research focused on identifying true crime podcast enthusiasts’ desire for other media outlets to explore their interests. We conducted five user interviews and distributed a 23-question online survey to help us determine our product specifications.

Survey Results

Would you rather visit macabre historical sites in-person or virtually?


We posted our survey on our personal social media accounts and reached out to individuals that we knew had an interest in entertainment-based true crime. One of our team members attempted to post the survey on a true crime subreddit, though quickly discovered that people with a heavy interest in solving true crime took offense at the idea of it being used as entertainment. Because of that response, we adapted our target audience to be a more casual fan and took a turn towards supernatural events as opposed to crime events.

Interviews

Key findings:

  • Interested in local historical content
  • Desire to hear other users’ experiences
  • Concerned about the treatment of victims and their families

While our interviews confirmed our instincts that true crime enthusiasts are eager to visit historical sites in-person, they stressed responsible treatment of victims and their families. Given this research, ensuring an ethical approach to disseminating true crime information became a priority.

User Persona

Based on our research, we developed our user persona, Alex. She is a casual true crime enthusiast and history buff that wants technologically innovative ways to engage with her interests and her community.

Storyboard

We see a young woman, Alex, sitting at her desk, listening to the latest episode of her favorite true crime podcast. She seems fascinated by the macabre and loves learning about dark history.

Alex is scrolling through her phone when she discovers a new app called "PeculiAR". Intrigued, she downloads it and starts to explore. As soon as she launches the app, Alex is transported to a different world. 

She walks outside and holds up her phone. She sees a dot on her phone hovering over the spooky church just up the street from her. As she approaches, she is prompted to click the bubble and what is revealed is darker than she could have hoped for.

Using the app, Alex visits local landmarks such as an old abandoned prison, a haunted cemetery, and a spooky old mansion. She is amazed by the details and the level of immersion the app provides.

During her journey, Alex discovers dark secrets about her city's history that she never knew before. She learns about unsolved murders, hidden graves, and forgotten tragedies and has much to talk about with her fellow true crime junkies.

As her latest journey comes to an end, Alex feels like she has a better understanding of her city's history and is excited to continue her exploration. She closes the app, but not before she notices another bubble in the distance. There's still more to discover…

User Testing & Results

AB Testing

79% of voters favored the header without the shopping cart, stating:

  • Competes with other buttons
  • Seems odd on a community website
  • Looks cleaner without

89% of voters favored larger text on the button, stating that it was:

  • CEasier to see
  • Larger call to action

Usability Testing

Objectives

  1. Sign up for a basic membership plan, receive a sign-up completion notification.
  2. Find the next meeting time and date.
  3. Find the member price for Frank’s honey soap.
  4. Find the link for the Scientific Beekeeping website.

Out of five user tests:

  • 100% success rate on 3 out of 4 tasks.
  • One unsuccessful attempt of objective 4 led us to iterate on our honeycomb buttons by adding text directing users to click the icons for more information.

Final Thoughts

This UX case study highlights the importance of a collaborative approach to UX design. Through the process of creating reusable elements and a comprehensive style guide, we were able to increase efficiency and consistency in our design work. However, it was the insight gained from multiple viewpoints, including those of stakeholders, that truly elevated our design solutions. By acknowledging and incorporating stakeholder needs, we were able to create a more user-centered design and ensure that our design decisions aligned with business goals. Ultimately, this case study demonstrates the value of teamwork and collaboration in achieving successful UX design outcomes.
