Web Accessibility Redesign

Web accessibility is a crucial aspect of design, and nowhere is it more important than in healthcare settings. My client, a therapist specializing in ecological anxiety, asked me to update her website to comply with ADA accessible design standards and edit her content to be more user-friendly. Her ultimate goals were increased usability, legal compliance, and ultimately, increased website traffic.

Analysis & Editing

The process began by analyzing the existing state of my client’s website and identifying what needed to be changed. I started with a basic heuristic analysis, where I identified pain points and inaccessible features, and then moved to editing down content to improve overall user experience.

I created a wireframe to outline the changes that would be made. I condensed the client stories into a single tab block to promote consistency and took out text blocks that appeared on other pages. My client suggested some alternate tag line options, so I sent her a few iterations with different tag lines and let her pick the one that she liked best.

Content Editing

Although the redesign was focused on the homepage, we determined that content editing for the whole site would greatly improve usability. For efficient content editing that could be easily reviewed by both parties, I copied all website text into a shared Google Doc. We created a key to indicate who was suggesting which edits and took turns reviewing the content. I was able to suggest combining some pages with repetitive content and my client took out blocks of text that were not necessary to get her message across.



Design Implementation

FAQ

The first place I started in implementing the new design was the FAQ. Initially all of the questions were grouped together, so I broke them up into labelled sections to improve readability. I edited the question content as we had determined in the last step, and then added headings to break up each section.

I implemented the proposed design changes to the homepage. I standardized the headings, changed the tagline, combined the client stories into one tab section, and made it stylistically consistent. I was also able to cut down some of the content chunks at this stage.

Outdoor Therapy/Workshops

The “Outdoor Therapy” and “Outdoor Workshops” pages had large sections of repetitive content, so I proposed a single page that covered both services. I took out the repeated content and added an optional workshop calendar that my client can enable when she schedules future group workshops.



Accessibility

My client’s main goal for her website redesign was to comply to accessibility standards. I focused on ensuring the website was visually accessible, usable though keyboard interactions, and passed though a commercial accessibility checker.

Color Accessibility

The primary accent color did not pass AA level contrast when used as a background for white text. Changing it to a slightly darker shade allowed for a better contrast ratio without compromising the overall visual design.

Skip Buttons

To allow for simple keyboard navigation, I added skip to content and skip to navigation to the top of each page.

Font Size

I added a font size toggler under the primary navigation bar. This gives users a simple option for visual accessibility without relying on their own browser plugins.

Tabindex

As the next step in keyboard navigation, I made sure each interactive feature had a tabindex value. The premade tab widget that had been used in the Wordpress theme did not have this enabled automatically, but I was able to input my own values into the heading of each tab.

Services and Specialties

Unfortunately I wasn’t able to create a keyboard accessible dropdown menu without entirely changing the Wordpress theme used on the site. The next best option was creating pages with the dropdown menu options as keyboard accessible buttons for each category. The site is now entirely keyboard accessible!



Final Iteration & Results

Form Entries

My client has chosen to disable web analytics on her site to maximize client privacy. Therefore, the best metric I have for tracking my design’s performance is the number of new form entries received. The design was launched in early August, and since then the website has consistently received more form entries than before, indicating a positive trend.

Final Thoughts

This was the first time actualizing my design process and I learned a lot. I felt very confident at the beginning given that the actual redesign was relatively simple. However, working with Wordpress came with a steep learning curve. I would want to spend some more time working with it and learning the technicalities of it before beginning another project based in Wordpress. I had a good idea of accessibility standards prior to starting as well, but learned quite a bit along the way and still feel I have more to learn. This project strengthened my passion for accessible technology design. I hope to have more opportunities in the future to expand my knowledge in this area!
