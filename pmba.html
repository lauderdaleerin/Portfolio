Website Redesign

The Portland Metro Beekeepers Association (PMBA) is a local organization created to connect beekeepers with community resources and advocate for environmental justice. Our group was inspired to redesign their online presence in an effort to increase web accessibility and increase association membership.

Research

Our research included online surveys, user interviews, a stakeholder interview, and a heuristic analysis to identify points that needed alteration on the existing PMBA website.

Survey Results

What was the greatest factor in determining why you joined a beekeepers association?


We posted our survey on beekeeping subreddits to engage with both professional and amateur beekeepers. Our main takeaways were users’ interest in educational resources and desire for an engaging community.

Heuristic Analysis

Hover over dots to view

Plain links on the cluttered background are difficult to read.

Patch logo design is cute, but it doesn’t scale down well and the spacing is not consistent

Odd to have both a drop down menu bar and hamburger navigation.

Photos need a border or shadow to stand out against the background.

Large scale, full opacity image for a background makes the website look cluttered.

Fonts are used to draw attention, but are done so inconsistently.

When testing the existing site with our users, we found that the information was clearly presented but the aesthetics were lacking.

Stakeholder Insight

Gains

  • Wants to expand the organization
  • Has the drive to improve
  • Responsive to outside perspectives
  • Believes in the importance of the association

Pains

  • Focused on established members, not new ones
  • Members struggle with technology
  • Facebook page is closed
  • No newsletter

Talking to the PMBA communicator revealed that they were more concerned about current member experience than gaining new members, contrary to our assumptions. They noted interest in a public online storefront and member forum, both elements of interest by survey respondents as well.



Defintion & Synthesis

Affinity Diagram

The first place I started in implementing the new design was the FAQ. Initially all of the questions were grouped together, so I broke them up into labelled sections to improve readability. I edited the question content as we had determined in the last step, and then added headings to break up each section.

Problem Statement

“Amateur beekeepers need a convenient resource to access local beekeeping workshops and other in-person beekeeping events because education and mentorship are the most important resources to gain when joining a beekeeping society. Beekeeping associations want to provide education and community to beekeepers and need to increase membership to be most effective. How might we increase beekeeper society membership, especially among younger beekeepers, and most effectively provide members with access to these educational resources?”

Redesign Goals

Increase Community Engagement
  • Create an events page with accessible meeting minutes
  • Create a forum for community discussion

Increase Membership
  • Make membership benefits clearer and more appealing
  • Make applying for membership easier
  • Develop membership tiers

Increase Revenue
  • Make supplies and products available to non-members
  • Give shoppers savings incentive with membership

Increase Access to Resources
  • Make educational resources more robust 
  • Make educational resources easier to find and navigate

After compiling our data and determining what features would be feasible to prototype in our time frame, we defined the goals of our redesign- to increase membership, community engagement, revenue, and access to resources.

I created some logo options for my group’s consideration and we decided that the combination of the Oregon state border and beehive communicated the organization’s message clearly. It is simple and scalable for future merchandising and branding opportunities.

Style Guide

We wanted to keep beekeeping as the primary focus of the website so we designed elements around bees and honeycombs in our color palette. We wanted the website to be visually engaging but allow the information to be streamlined and accessible.



Prototyping

Form Entries

My client has chosen to disable web analytics on her site to maximize client privacy. Therefore, the best metric I have for tracking my design’s performance is the number of new form entries received. The design was launched in early August, and since then the website has consistently received more form entries than before, indicating a positive trend.

Future Steps

Although I am not planning on continuing to work directly on this project, I do hope to take the following steps to expand my knowledge in these areas:

Final Thoughts

This was the first time actualizing my design process and I learned a lot. I felt very confident at the beginning given that the actual redesign was relatively simple. However, working with Wordpress came with a steep learning curve. I would want to spend some more time working with it and learning the technicalities of it before beginning another project based in Wordpress. I had a good idea of accessibility standards prior to starting as well, but learned quite a bit along the way and still feel I have more to learn. This project strengthened my passion for accessible technology design. I hope to have more opportunities in the future to expand my knowledge in this area!
