Web Redesign Case Study

The Portland Metro Beekeepers Association (PMBA) is a local organization created to connect beekeepers with community resources and advocate for environmental justice. Our group was inspired to redesign their online presence in an effort to increase web accessibility and increase association membership.

Research

Our research included online surveys, user interviews, a stakeholder interview, and a heuristic analysis to identify points that needed alteration on the existing PMBA website.

Stakeholder Insight

Gains

  • Wants to expand the organization
  • Has the drive to improve
  • Responsive to outside perspectives
  • Believes in the importance of the association

Pains

  • Focused on established members, not new ones
  • Members struggle with technology
  • Facebook page is closed
  • No newsletter

Talking to the PMBA communicator revealed that they were more concerned about current member experience than gaining new members, contrary to our assumptions. They noted interest in a public online storefront and member forum, both elements of interest by survey respondents as well.

Definition & Synthesis

Affinity Diagram

After compiling our interview and survey data, it was clear that people’s interest in joining beekeeping associations was primarily to connect with others and to find information and resources on beekeeping.

Problem Statement

“Amateur beekeepers are looking for education and mentorship by joining a beekeeping society to access local beekeeping workshops and other in-person events. Beekeeping associations want to provide education and community to beekeepers and need to increase membership to be most effective. How might we increase beekeeper society membership, especially among younger beekeepers, and most effectively provide members with access to these educational resources?”

Storyboard

One day, while Patricia is working in her garden, she hears a faint buzzing. She spots from across the yard a large swarm of bees in her tree on her property. She is a little scared but she knows that bees are important to our environment and knows that she doesn't want to harm them, she just doesn't want them in her yard.

She takes to her computer and starts by searching for local bee removal services. She finds a local Beekeeping Association that has a hotline specifically for swarms and gives them a call.

The next day an amateur beekeeper named Scott comes to her home and carefully removes the swarm. He tells Patricia that they will be going to a local apiary with another member of the Portland Metro Beekeeping Association who frequently volunteers to rescue swarms.

Over the next few days, Patricia is thinking about all of the things Scott told her about bees. He really seemed to love them and knew quite a lot about them. She knows that they are important for our ecosystem and wants to learn a little more...and maybe talk to someone about keeping her own bees.

She logs onto the Portland Metro Beekeeping association like Scott recommended and reads lots of very informative and interesting facts about bees and beekeeping. She starts to think that maybe it wouldn't be soo hard for her to start keeping bees for herself.

Patricia decides that she wants to learn more about keeping bees and to stay up to date with all the information her local association may publish. She also wants to support the work the volunteers like Scott do, so she signs up to become a member.

Redesign Goals

Increase Community Engagement
  • Create an events page with accessible meeting minutes
  • Create a forum for community discussion
Increase Membership
  • Make membership benefits clearer and more appealing
  • Make applying for membership easier
  • Develop membership tiers
Increase Revenue
  • Make supplies and products available to non-members
  • Give shoppers savings incentive with membership
Increase Access to Resources
  • Make educational resources more robust 
  • Improve navigation for educational resources

After compiling our data and determining what features would be feasible to prototype in our time frame, we defined the goals of our redesign- to increase membership, community engagement, revenue, and access to resources.

Style Guide

We wanted to keep beekeeping as the primary focus of the website so we designed elements around bees and honeycombs in our color palette. We wanted the website to be visually engaging but allow the information to be streamlined and accessible.

User Testing & Results

AB Testing

79% of voters favored the header without the shopping cart, stating:

  • Competes with other buttons
  • Seems odd on a community-focused website
  • Looks cleaner without it

89% of voters favored larger text on the button, stating that it was:

  • Easier to see
  • A larger call to action

Usability Testing

Objectives

  1. Sign up for a basic membership plan, receive a sign-up completion notification
  2. Find the next meeting time and date

    5. Out of five user tests:

      7. Final Thoughts

      This UX case study highlights the importance of a collaborative approach to UX design. Through the process of creating reusable elements and a comprehensive style guide, we were able to increase efficiency and consistency in our design work. However, it was the insight gained from multiple viewpoints, including those of stakeholders, that truly elevated our design solutions. By acknowledging and incorporating stakeholder needs, we were able to create a more user-centered design and ensure that our design decisions aligned with business goals. Ultimately, this case study demonstrates the value of teamwork and collaboration in achieving successful UX design outcomes.
